Step-by-Step Guide

1

Get Your GitHub Site Ready

Go to your GitHub repository and follow these clicks:

  • Settings ‚Üí Pages
  • Type your domain (e.g., example.com)
  • Click Save
2

Visit Your Domain Store

Log in to where you bought your name (GoDaddy, Namecheap, Google Domains, etc.). Look for "DNS Settings".

3

Add the Address Cards

For the main address (@): Add four "A" records.

Type: A | Name: @ | Value: 185.199.108.153
Type: A | Name: @ | Value: 185.199.109.153
Type: A | Name: @ | Value: 185.199.110.153
Type: A | Name: @ | Value: 185.199.111.153

For the www version: Add one "CNAME".

Type: CNAME | Name: www | Value: YOUR-USERNAME.github.io
4

Wait for the Mailman

The internet mailman is slow! üê¢ It can take 5 minutes to 48 hours to deliver your new address cards everywhere.

5

The Safety Lock

Risks Explained for Toddlers

üéà The Wildcard Trap

Don't use the * star! It's like leaving all your toys outside for anyone to take. Bad people could use your domain for things you don't want.

üîê The HTTPS Lock

Always click "Enforce HTTPS". Without it, people can peek at your secret messages and passwords. Keep your door locked!

üìù The Forgotten Note

Never delete the CNAME file in your repository. It's the note that tells people where your house is!

Helper Tool

DNS Command Generator

dig example.com +nostats +nocomments +nocmd

Copy this into your "Terminal" or "Command Prompt" to see if the address cards are working!
